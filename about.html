The Story Behind BiyteLüm

BiyteLüm sits at an unusual intersection: privacy and AI compliance consulting, and expert legal advisory for immigration cases. To understand why these two service lines exist under one roof, you need to understand what connects them.

Our founder spent over six years as a federal adjudications officer at US Citizenship and Immigration Services — processing asylum applications, refugee petitions, and humanitarian protection cases. Every case involved some of the most sensitive personal data that exists: persecution histories, medical records, family separation details, biometric information, criminal background data. That data, if mishandled, could expose individuals to deportation, persecution, or worse.

"Asylum adjudication and privacy compliance share the same core: protecting vulnerable people's data from misuse with serious real-world consequences."

That experience created something no certification program can replicate: a deep, practical understanding of what it means to handle sensitive human data under high-stakes conditions with serious regulatory accountability. The same precision that governed asylum adjudications now drives BiyteLüm's approach to privacy compliance and AI governance.

Today, BiyteLüm serves two audiences. Organizations navigating cross-border data privacy and AI governance obligations — particularly those operating between the US, EU, and Latin America — benefit from our CIPP/E, CIPM, and AI Agent Security credentials and our registered US and EU entities. Immigration attorneys handling asylum and humanitarian cases benefit from direct insider knowledge of USCIS adjudication standards that no academic or policy expert can offer.
